# Carrier Management Expertise

This skill provides comprehensive knowledge of carrier relationship management, rate negotiations, performance tracking, and capacity planning for freight forwarders.

## Carrier Landscape

### Ocean Carrier Market Structure

**Market Concentration:**
- Top 10 carriers control ~85% of global capacity
- Alliance structures dominate trade lanes
- Consolidation trend continues

**Current Alliances (2024):**
| Alliance | Members | Global Share |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| 2M (dissolved 2025) | Maersk, MSC | ~35% |
| Ocean Alliance | CMA CGM, COSCO, Evergreen, OOCL | ~30% |
| THE Alliance | Hapag-Lloyd, ONE, Yang Ming, HMM | ~20% |
| Independent | MSC, Maersk (post-2M), ZIM | ~15% |

### Carrier Service Types

| Service | Description | Premium |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Standard | Regular sailing schedule | Base rate |
| Premium/Guaranteed | Priority loading, faster transit | +20-40% |
| Spot | One-time booking at market rate | Variable |
| Contract | Committed volume at fixed rate | -10-30% |

## Rate Structures

### Rate Components

| Component | Type | Variability |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Base Ocean Freight | Per container | Negotiable |
| Bunker Adjustment (BAF) | Per container | Weekly/Monthly adjustment |
| Currency Adjustment (CAF) | Percentage | Monthly adjustment |
| Terminal Handling (THC) | Per container | Fixed by port |
| Peak Season Surcharge (PSS) | Per container | Seasonal |
| Equipment Imbalance | Per container | Route dependent |
| Low Sulfur Surcharge (LSS) | Per container | Fuel dependent |
| War Risk Surcharge | Per container | Route dependent |

### Rate Types

**Contract Rates:**
| Type | Duration | Volume Commitment | Flexibility |
|------|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Annual Contract | 12 months | High (MQC) | Low |
| Quarterly Contract | 3 months | Medium | Medium |
| FAK (Freight All Kinds) | Varies | Commodity-agnostic | Medium |
| Named Account (NAC) | 12 months | Named shipper specific | Low |

**Spot Rates:**
- No volume commitment
- Valid for single shipment
- Market-driven pricing
- Higher than contract rates (usually)

### Minimum Quantity Commitment (MQC)

**MQC Structure:**
```
Total Contract Volume: 1,000 TEU/year
Quarterly MQC: 250 TEU
Shortfall Penalty: $100/TEU not shipped
Overage: Subject to space availability
```

**Best Practices:**
- Commit to 80% of expected volume
- Build in flexibility clauses
- Include force majeure provisions
- Negotiate shortfall penalties

## Rate Negotiation

### Preparation

**Data to Gather:**
1. Historical volume by trade lane
2. Current rates vs market benchmarks
3. Carrier performance scorecards
4. Forecast volume by quarter
5. Service requirements (transit time, equipment)

**Market Intelligence:**
| Source | Data |
|--------|------|
| Freightos FBX | Spot rate indices |
| Drewry WCI | Container rate benchmarks |
| Xeneta | Contract rate analytics |
| Carrier Rate Sheets | Published tariffs |
| Industry Publications | Market commentary |

### Negotiation Strategies

**Leverage Points:**
| Factor | Impact on Negotiation |
|--------|----------------------|
| Volume size | Higher volume = better rates |
| Volume consistency | Predictable shipping = carrier preference |
| Multi-trade lane | Bundling routes increases leverage |
| Equipment balance | Backhaul volume is valuable |
| Peak/off-peak mix | Off-peak volume is valuable |
| Payment terms | Quick pay can earn discounts |

**Tactics:**
| Strategy | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| Competitive bidding | Multiple carrier options |
| Incumbent advantage | Strong existing relationship |
| Service bundling | Need multiple trade lanes |
| Volume aggregation | Combine with partners |
| Long-term commitment | Stable market, reliable carrier |
| Short-term flexibility | Volatile market |

### Rate Benchmarking

**Benchmark Categories:**
| Category | Comparison |
|----------|------------|
| Market index | vs Freightos/Drewry |
| Historical | vs prior year rates |
| Competitor | vs industry peers |
| All-in cost | Total landed cost comparison |

**Analysis Framework:**
```
Rate Competitiveness Score = (Market Rate - Your Rate) / Market Rate × 100

Score > 10%: Excellent rates
Score 0-10%: Good rates
Score -10-0%: Average rates
Score < -10%: Below market - renegotiate
```

## Capacity Management

### Allocation Planning

**Allocation Strategy:**
| Carrier Tier | Allocation % | Rationale |
|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| Primary (1-2) | 60-70% | Best rates, reliability |
| Secondary (2-3) | 20-30% | Backup, competition |
| Spot | 10% | Flexibility |

### Capacity Constraints

**Common Causes:**
| Cause | Impact | Mitigation |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Peak season | Space shortages, rolling | Book early, use premium |
| Port congestion | Delays, blank sailings | Alternative ports |
| Equipment imbalance | Container shortages | Reposition, shipper-owned |
| Blank sailings | Reduced capacity | Multi-carrier strategy |
| Vessel delays | Schedule disruption | Buffer time in planning |

### Space Protection

**Guaranteed Space Programs:**
- Premium service with guaranteed loading
- Typically 20-40% above standard rates
- Useful for time-sensitive cargo
- May include equipment guarantee

**Booking Best Practices:**
| Timing | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| Peak season | Book 3-4 weeks ahead |
| Standard | Book 1-2 weeks ahead |
| Spot market | Book ASAP when rates favorable |
| Contract | Use allocation regularly to maintain |

|--------------|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Operational | Weekly | Operations teams | Issues, upcoming shipments |
| Business Review | Quarterly | Management | Performance, rates, forecast |
| Strategic | Annual | Executives | Partnership, long-term plans |

### Quarterly Business Review Agenda

```
QUARTERLY BUSINESS REVIEW
=========================

1. Performance Review (30 min)
   - KPI scorecard presentation
   - Issue review and resolution status
   - Service quality trends

2. Volume Analysis (20 min)
   - Actual vs committed volume
   - Volume by trade lane
   - Forecast for next quarter

3. Market Discussion (15 min)
   - Rate trends
   - Capacity outlook
   - Industry developments

4. Operational Topics (20 min)
   - Process improvements
   - System integrations
   - Equipment needs

5. Action Items (15 min)
   - Open issues resolution
   - Next steps
   - Follow-up schedule
```

## Contract Management

### Key Contract Terms

| Term | Importance | Negotiation Points |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Rate validity | High | Duration, adjustment triggers |
| MQC/MAQ | High | Volume flexibility, shortfall penalties |
| Surcharges | High | Caps, adjustment frequency |
| Equipment | Medium | Guarantee, detention free time |
| Space | Medium | Allocation, peak season protection |
| Payment | Medium | Terms, currency |
| Service level | Medium | Transit time, reliability guarantees |
| Force majeure | Low | Scope of exceptions |

### Contract Checklist

**Before Signing:**
- [ ] Rate comparison to market complete
- [ ] Volume commitment achievable
- [ ] Surcharge structure understood
- [ ] Service levels meet requirements
- [ ] Payment terms acceptable
- [ ] Exit/amendment clauses reviewed
- [ ] Legal review completed
- [ ] Operational team aligned

### Rate Amendment Process

**Triggers for Amendment:**
- Market rate changes >10%
- Fuel price changes beyond cap
- Currency fluctuations beyond band
- Volume commitment shortfall
- Service performance issues

**Process:**
1. Monitor trigger conditions
2. Document justification
3. Propose amendment terms
4. Negotiate adjustments
5. Execute amendment
6. Update systems
